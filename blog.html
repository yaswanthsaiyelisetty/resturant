The Secrets Behind the Perfect Andhra Biryani

What makes Andhra biryani different from Hyderabadi or Lucknowi? It's all in the spice — the bold use of green chilies, the generous hand with whole spices, and the slow dum cooking that allows every grain of rice to absorb the essence of the meat. At Eat Heat, our biryani recipe has been passed down through three generations.

The secret starts with choosing the right basmati — aged, fragrant, and long-grained. We toast our whole spices fresh every morning, grind our own masala blends, and marinate our meats overnight in yogurt and Andhra red chili paste. The dum process seals in the flavors, creating a biryani that opens like a treasure chest when served at your table.

Gongura: The Tangy Soul of Andhra Cuisine

Gongura, or sorrel leaf, is the crown jewel of Andhra cooking. This tangy, slightly sour green leaf transforms any dish it touches into something extraordinary. At Eat Heat, our Gongura Chicken is one of the most requested dishes — fiery, tangy, and deeply flavorful.

The leaf itself is packed with vitamins and has been used in Telugu households for centuries. We source our gongura from local farms in the Palnadu region, ensuring freshness and authentic flavor. Whether pickled, cooked into curries, or used in chutneys, gongura is truly the soul of Andhra food.

Pesarattu: Andhra's Beloved Protein-Rich Breakfast

Before protein shakes and superfood bowls became trendy, Telugu families had the Pesarattu — a crispy crepe made from green moong dal, naturally high in protein and fiber. This simple yet brilliant breakfast has been a staple in Andhra homes for generations.

At Eat Heat, we serve our Pesarattu stuffed with upma and paired with allam (ginger) chutney that has a spicy kick. It's the perfect way to start a day of exploring Narasaraopet, or simply a satisfying lunch that's both healthy and utterly delicious.
